MERCHANTS MANAGEMENT SPECIFICATION
1. Purpose

The Merchants page provides administrators with structured oversight of all merchant accounts on the platform. It supports business verification, approval workflows, and ongoing risk and compliance management for entities processing transactions.

2. Target Users

Compliance and verification officers
Operations team
Risk and fraud analysts
Finance and settlement administrators

Access must be governed by role-based permissions.

3. Core Objectives

Maintain a complete registry of all merchant accounts
Enable efficient merchant verification and approval workflows
Provide clear visibility into business legitimacy and operational status
Support regulatory compliance and transaction risk management

4. Information Architecture

The Merchants section consists of two primary views:

Merchant List View
Merchant Detail View

Context such as filters and navigation state must be preserved between views.

5. Merchant List View
Purpose

Provide a high-level, searchable overview of all registered merchants.

Merchant Table

Each row represents a merchant and must display:

Business name

Merchant ID

Registration date

Verification status

Account status

Primary action entry point

Search and Filtering

Search
Supports lookup by:

Business name

Merchant ID

Registered email or phone number

Search must return results in near real time.

Filters
Available filters include:

Verification status (pending, approved, rejected)

Account status (active, suspended)

Business category or industry

Filters must be combinable.

Sorting

Default sort order is newest merchants first.
Optional sorting by business name, registration date, or verification status.

6. Merchant Detail View
Purpose

Provide a comprehensive view of a merchantâ€™s business profile and verification state.

Business Profile Section

Business name and legal entity type
Registration number and issuing authority
Tax identification details where applicable
Registered business address
Primary contact person and contact details

All fields must reflect the latest submitted and verified data.

Verification and Compliance Section

Submitted Documentation
Business registration certificates
Tax compliance documents
Proof of address
Authorized representative identification

Verification Status
Pending
Approved
Rejected

Approval Workflow

Approve Merchant
Grants transactional and settlement privileges
Requires confirmation and administrator authentication

Reject Merchant
Requires mandatory rejection reason
May allow resubmission depending on policy

All decisions must be logged with:

Administrator identity

Timestamp

Decision reason

7. Account Status Controls

Active
Merchant can process transactions normally.

Suspended
Transactions are blocked due to compliance or risk concerns.

State changes must be immediate and fully auditable.


8. Interaction and Safety Rules

Approval and rejection actions require explicit confirmation.
Bulk approvals are restricted or disabled.
Rejected or suspended merchants must be clearly flagged across the system.

9. Performance and Scalability

Merchant lists must support large volumes with pagination or virtualization.
Filtering and search must remain responsive under operational load.

10. Accessibility and Design Requirements

All interactive elements must meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards.
Status indicators must include text labels in addition to color cues.
Layouts must follow the Design System Foundation for spacing, typography, and color semantics.

11. Success Criteria

Administrators can quickly verify merchant legitimacy.
Approval workflows are clear, controlled, and auditable.
Risk actions are executed confidently and consistently.
