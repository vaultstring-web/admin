1. Purpose

The Customers page provides administrators and compliance officers with full visibility and control over customer accounts. It enables identity verification management, account status enforcement, and detailed customer inspection in support of regulatory compliance, fraud prevention, and operational oversight.

2. Target Users

Compliance and KYC officers
Operations team
Risk and fraud analysts
System administrators

Access must be restricted based on role and permission level.

3. Core Objectives

Maintain a complete and searchable registry of customers
Streamline KYC review and approval workflows
Enable rapid identification of high-risk or non-compliant users
Provide clear visibility into customer account status and history

4. Information Architecture

The Customers section is structured into two primary views:

Customer List View
Customer Detail View

Navigation between views must preserve context and filters.

5. Customer List View
Purpose

Provide a scalable, high-level view of all registered customers.

Core Features

Customer Table
Each row represents a single customer and must display:

Full name

Unique customer ID

Registration date

KYC status

Account status (active or blocked)

Primary action entry point

Search and Filtering

Search
Supports keyword-based search across:

Name

Customer ID

Email or phone number

Search must return results in near real time.

Filters
Available filters include:

KYC status (pending, approved, rejected)

Account status (active, blocked)

Registration date range

Multiple filters must be combinable.

Sorting

Default sort order is most recently registered customers first.
Users may sort by name, registration date, or KYC status.

6. Customer Detail View
Purpose

Provide a comprehensive profile of an individual customer.

Profile Information

Basic identity information
Contact details
Account creation date
Current KYC status
Account status

All displayed data must reflect the latest verified source.

KYC Review Section

KYC Data Display
Uploaded identity documents
Selfie or biometric verification where applicable
Submitted personal information

KYC Actions
Approve KYC
Reject KYC with mandatory rejection reason

All actions must be logged with:

Administrator identity

Timestamp

Decision rationale

Account Control Actions

Block Account
Prevents all transactional activity
Requires confirmation and reason

Unblock Account
Restores transactional access
Requires appropriate authorization

State changes must be immediate and auditable.

7. Status Definitions

KYC Status
Pending: Submitted and awaiting review
Approved: Fully verified and compliant
Rejected: Verification failed or insufficient

Account Status
Active: Full platform access
Blocked: Access restricted due to risk or violation

Statuses must be clearly distinguishable visually and textually.


8. Interaction and Safety Rules

Destructive actions require confirmation.
Bulk actions are restricted or disabled for high-risk operations.
Blocked customers must be visually flagged across the platform.

9. Performance and Scalability

The customer list must support large datasets with pagination or virtualization.
Search and filters must remain responsive under load.

10. Accessibility and Design Requirements

All controls must meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards.
Status indicators must include text labels.
Layouts must follow the Design System Foundation for typography, spacing, and color usage.

11. Success Criteria

Administrators can locate a customer within seconds.
KYC decisions are traceable and defensible.
Risk actions are executed confidently without ambiguity.
